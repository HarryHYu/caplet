# Legal and school-trust release gate

The product implements plain-language terms, privacy controls, guardian consent, child-safe defaults, report handling, data export/deletion and a visible processor list. Code completion is not a substitute for confirming the operator's real legal and procurement details.

Before a public Australian school launch, the release owner must record evidence for every item below:

- Australian counsel has reviewed the current `/terms` and `/trust` text, child-consent flow, classroom moderation process, financial-education disclaimers and affiliate disclosures.
- The service operator's exact legal name, ABN/ACN if applicable, physical/service address and privacy contact are added to the public notice and contracting documents.
- A dated subprocessor register confirms provider, purpose, data categories, contractual role, production hosting region and likely overseas countries for Railway, Vercel, S3-compatible storage, OpenAI, Google and Resend.
- A privacy impact assessment covers children, AI-submitted text, classroom evidence, free text, profiles, uploads, analytics, data exports, erasure and backup lifecycle.
- A child-safety owner and an independent administrator are rostered for escalated reports; the overdue queue and contact route have been exercised with a non-production drill.
- School terms define controller/processor responsibilities, teacher verification, guardian notices, retention, incident notification, support and offboarding/export.
- The production backup bucket has versioning, KMS encryption and a documented lifecycle. A clean restore drill and account-erasure test both have current evidence.
- Marketing, pricing, curriculum, accessibility and performance claims have an evidence owner and review date. Sponsored and affiliate listings remain clearly labelled at the point of action.

Release approval should link to the counsel sign-off, privacy impact assessment, subprocessor register, safety drill, backup verification and test run. Do not replace missing evidence with an unchecked assertion in product copy.
