Subject: Chemistry (Year 11) — Sample marking guidelines

Question Q1: Explain why ionic compounds conduct electricity when molten but not when solid. (3 marks)

Marking criteria:
- 1 mark: States that ionic compounds contain charged particles (ions).
- 1 mark: Explains that in the solid state ions are held in a fixed lattice and cannot move.
- 1 mark: Explains that when molten the ions are free to move and carry charge, allowing conduction.

Exemplar (3/3) answer:
Ionic compounds are made of positive and negative ions. In the solid state these ions are locked in a rigid lattice and cannot move, so no charge can flow. When the compound is melted, the lattice breaks down and the ions become free to move, so they can carry electric charge and the molten compound conducts electricity.

Common errors:
- Saying electrons carry the charge (it is the ions that move in ionic conduction).
- Stating ions "move more when hot" without linking movement to charge transport.

Question Q2: Define first ionisation energy. (1 mark)

Marking criteria:
- 1 mark: The energy required to remove one mole of electrons (the outermost electron) from one mole of gaseous atoms to form one mole of gaseous 1+ ions.
